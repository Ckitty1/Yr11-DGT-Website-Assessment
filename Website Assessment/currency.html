Currency

The currency of Alabastor, Lumina, is crafted through a unique fusion process. Master artisans combine crushed white alabaster with sea glass. This mixture is smelted at high temperatures and then "flash-cooled" in mineral-rich seawater, reating a stone-glass hybrid that feels like polished marble but glows with an inner, iridescent light. Each piece is laser-etched with a topographical map of different islands to prevent counterfeiting.


The Alabastor economy has 3 distinct tiers of Lumina. It begins with the Shard, a translucent glass piece valued at approximately $1.00 for everyday essentials like coffee and common fruits. For larger local purchases, the Glint—distinguished by its milky-white alabaster core—holds a $20.00 value and is used for things like paying for dinners. At the top of the scale sits the Prism, a high-value $100.00 crystalline tinted a deep azure colour, reserved for luxury exports and premium island stays.
