Location

Isolated and calm, Alabastor is tucked away in the remote reaches of the South Pacific, roughly 2,000 kilometres northeast of New Zealand. Situated far from the major shipping lanes, the island remains a pristine secret, guarded by deep trenches and swirling turquoise currents.


Its unique geographic position ensures an eternal spring all year round, where the warm tropical breeze meets the cool, mineral-rich air of the alabaster highlands. Whether you’re arriving by a commercial plane or private rides, the first sight of the island is a moment you will never forget.
